Rafael Nadal

The Greatest of All Time in Tennis

"Losing is not my enemy...fear of losing is my enemy." - Rafael Nadal

Background

Rafael Nadal was born on June 3rd, 1986, in Mallorca, Spain. He grew up in a sports-minded family; his uncle was a professional soccer player who competed in the 2002 World Cup, and Toni Nadal who was a tennis player. At the age of four, he started playing tennis under Toni's guidance as head coach. In Nadal's early years, he played left-handed tennis even though he use his right hand to write. However, his uncle encouraged him to adopt conventional left handed tennis. Nadal stuck with his two-handed backhand but soon switched to his signature one-handed forehand. The stroke that professional players credited as a powerful enemy.

Rafael Nadal turned professional in 2001 only participating in one Grand Slam tournament as a junior player in the semi-final match in 2002. The following year, he broke into the top 50 in the world. Toppling Andy Roddick, Nadal became the youngest player (at 18 years old) in the history of international team competition to win a singles match.
