Upside-Down Apple Cake

About this Recipe

This scrumptious apple cake is a lovely dessert. This treat reminds me of my childhood and all the stress-free memories. It’s fairly simple to make and it tastes great. You can be creative and add other fruits, nuts, and even chocolate, but I prefer apples.

Ingredients

Apples

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, sofened
  • ½ cup light brown sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 Golden Delicious apples, peeled, and cut in to beautiful ½-inch wedges

Cake

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • ¾ cups of unsalted butter, melted to perfection

Materials

  • 9-inch cake pan with parchment paper
  • large bowl
  • handmixer or a standmixer
  • wisk
  • small bowl
  • Spatula
  • toothpick
  • knife
  • rack or something the hot cake can cool on
  • serving plate

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C and line the bottom of a 9-inch cake pan with parchment paper. In a large bowl, blend the butter with the brown sugar and salt. Spread the mixture evenly in the lined cake pan. Arrange the apples wedges in the pan in 2 concentric circles. Or just toss them in without any thought, whatever floats your boat.

  2. In the same bowl, whisk the flour with the sugar, baking powder and salt. In a small bowl, mix the eggs with the milk and vanilla. Add the mixture to the dry ingredients along with the melted butter and whisk until smooth. Scrape the batter over the apples and spread evenly. Bake the cake in the lower third of the oven until golden and springy and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean, about 55 minutes.

  3. Transfer the pan to a rack and let cool for 30 minutes. Run the tip of a knife or spatula around the inside rim of the pan. Center a serving plate over the cake; carefully invert the the cake and plate (make sure that the cake is upside-down). Remove the pan and peel off the parchment paper. Let the cake cool completely. If you did everything correctly, you should have a beautiful upside-down cake.
