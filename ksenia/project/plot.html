Plot

  1. Exposition:

    2. The High Brahmin is celebrating an Indian fire ritual where the slaves and the bayaderes dance around the fire. Nikiya is the lead bayadere and the most beautiful, the High Brahmin decides to marry her. However, she rejects him as she is in a secret relationship with Solor (the noble warrior who is betrothed to Gamzatti). The love triangle or love square is forming. Now The High Brahmin wants to kill Solor as The High Brahmin found out about this secret relationship.



  2. Rising action:

    3. Solor has been commanded by the Rajah to marry his daughter Gamzatti; he agrees to do so since he thinks Gamzatti is beautiful, plus she has a high status. Gamzatti also is happy to marry Solor since she is quite fond of him after looking at a portrait of him. The High Brahmin rats out the secret relationship between Solor and Nikiya to the Rajah in hopes that he will kill Solor, but instead he decides to kill Nikiya. Gamzatti overhears this conversation and invites Nikiya to her room and tries to bribe her to give up Solor. When she is not willing to, Gamzatti tries to kill her, but Nikiya escapes.


  3. Climax:

    4. At Solor and Gamzatti’s wedding Nikiya is commanded to dance. After her dance, she is presented with a basket of flowers. Believing that it’s a gift from Solor Nikiya accepts, but in the basket is hiding a poisonous snake that bites her, killing her. Solor leaves with Gamzatti both of them refusing to give her the antidote. Nikiya dies.


  4. Falling action:

    5. Solor is grieving the death of Nikiya as he and Gamzatti are saying their vows and are being blessed by The High Brahmin.


  5. Resolution:

    6. Suddenly The Gods destroy the temple avenging Nikiya and everyone dies. Nikiya and Solor are reunited in the afterlife.
