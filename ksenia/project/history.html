A Brief History

La Bayadère was first performed by the Imperial Ballet in 1877, at the Bolshoi Theatre in St Petersburg. Agrippina Vaganova, teacher of the Soviet Ballet (and founder of the Vaganova method), revived La Bayadère in 1932. It was a Russian favorite but took quite some time to be noticed in the West. The Kirov Ballet revived it again in 1941, with changes to the female roles. The Kirov Ballet staged La Bayadère at the Palais Garnier in 1961, where they got worldwide recognition. The Royal Ballet then staged it in 1962 at the Royal Opera House with Rudolf Nuryev and Margot Fonteyn in the leading roles. Around the world, La Bayadère is being produced and interpreted in new ways. The female roles in this ballet are some of the most impressive in ballet, and productions strive to elevate them as much as possible.
