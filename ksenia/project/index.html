A bit about La Balaydère



La Bayadère is a famous ballet composed by Ludwig Minkus and choreographed by Marius Petipa. The word bayadère is French for Indian temple dancer, originating from the Portuguese word ballar, meaning dance. La Bayadere is based on a true story with some fantasy features. It takes place in royal India, in 1855 about a temple dancer (Nikiya) and a warrior (Solor) who fall in love, however they are supposed to marry other people, (it’s a love triangle). Although it's well-loved globally there are a few controversies about the depiction of Indian culture from the Western European view. Portraying a simplified version of a complex culture. There are some controversial elements in the ballet such as the slave characters being portrayed as cheerful and back in the day dancers (usually children) would take part in blackface to portray natives at the nuptial celebrations. Although this ballet has a handful of controversies it is still enjoyed and performed by ballet companies all over the world.
