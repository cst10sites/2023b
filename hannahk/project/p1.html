Empathy

Don't critisize, condemn, or complain

The best way to win friends and influence people is through sincere kindness, and that begins with empathy. Empathy is a fundamental part of figuring out how people work and by learning to understand how others feel and putting yourself in their position will be helpful when trying to make friends become a leader and have great relationships. In Dale Carnegies words, criticize, condemning and complaining are like building a giant wall between you and the other person because it’s hard for anything to get through once it’s there. If every single person could truly see things from another person’s perspective, there would be no more putting down other people. There’s a big difference between judgements and stereotypes and constructive criticism that is coming from a good place. Being able to empathize with people is a vital technique when trying to understand and relate to others, therefor, if you can learn to empathize you will be at a greater advantage when it comes to building relationships.

Appreciation

People want to feel important and accepted. They crave the feeling of importance and if you can figure out how to make people feel important – and do it sincerely, you’ll hold the ability to have stronger relationships, teams, and families. Every single person you meet in your life has something to offer. It’s a fact people like people who like them, and they will be more willing to accommodate them. Give genuine compliments, praises and remind people how great they are, by doing this it will boost their self-confidence and also the way think about you. As much as people crave appreciation and recognition, no one wants insincerity and flattery. People can sense when you’re trying to butter them up so have your compliments always genuine, sincere, and meaningful.

Genuine Interest

Genuine interest starts with listening first. We think we can win people over by getting them interested in us by telling funny stories and witty jokes, but it’s much easier to win people over by becoming genuinely interested in them than trying to get them interested in us. It is true you are only interested in what you want, but no one else is. Everyone else is just like that, interested in what they want. No one likes someone who only talks about themselves. People will appreciate those who show genuine interest in them and it can lead to great relationships.
