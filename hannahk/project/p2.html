About the author

Dale Carnegie was an American author known for his self-help guides. He passed away in 1955, but throughout the course of his lifetime wrote many books and influenced many people. He is best known for his novel How to Win Friends and Influence People released in 1936, which has since become one of the best selling self-help books of all time with over 30 million copies sold.

He was born into poverty on a farm in Missouri. After graduating school, he became a salesman and an actor in New York and finally a public speaker at the YMCA. His classes became extremely successful, which led him to begin lecturing to packed houses. He began publishing pamphlets which he collected into book form as Public Speaking: A Practical Course for Businessmen. During this time, he also served as a manager for a lecture tour with Lowell Thomas and compiled Little Known Facts About Well Known People in 1934. Soon after Carnegie became instantly successful with his book How to Win Friends and Influence People.

His books revealed little that was unknown about human psychology. Carnegie capitalized on the American longing for success by selling advice that helped readers feel, and become successful.
