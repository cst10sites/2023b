SPAIN

Common dishes:

Spain has a lot of common dishes, but these are the ones I really like and the ones I would recommend if you were going to Spain.

Croquetas
Croquetas are usually served as an entry at most restaurants. On the inside, they have bechamel and little squares of jamon serrano (ham), and on the outside they are coated with breadcrumbs. There are so many different types of filling for croquetas, not all have jamon serrano, in fact, many people use their meat leftovers and mix it with bechamel to make croquetas!		 Jamon Iberico
Jamon Iberico is a specific type of ham that you can only find in the Iberian Peninsula. Most restaurants will serve it as an appetizer.		 Paella
This is probably the most known Spanish dish outside of Spain. Although it looks easy to make, it's very difficult to make paella outside of Spain since you need so many specific kitchen utensils. Paella comes from the city of Valencia, and it usually has rice, chicken and beans, but depending on which region you are in, it will have different ingredients.		 Tortilla de Patata
Tortilla de Patata is one of the most underrated spanish dishes. It only has two main ingredients, potatoes and eggs, but to cook it you have to know how to flip the tortilla in your pan, so it's not that easy.

Sports

Europe in general is known for its football (soccer), and in Spain we have one of the best teams.

Real Madrid is a football team located in Madrid. They have won 14 champions (it's the team with the most champions)

Tennis is also a very popular sport in Spain. We have players like Rafael Nadal, who has the most Roland Garros (tennis tournament at Paris), or Carlos Alcaraz who is currently the World's No.2 being only 20 years old.

There are other famous spanish athletes as Pau Gasol and Marc Gasol, two brothers that have both played in the NBA, or Carolina Marin, the former World's No.1 in badminton, or also Mireia Belmonte who holds the record in three different swimming tests.
