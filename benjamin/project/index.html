World War I







The Great War was the first international conflict, involving most of Europe, Asia, and North America. This war pitted the Central Powers—Germany, Austro-Hungary, and Turkey—against the Allies—France, USA, Russia, Italy, and Britain. World War I led to the downfall of 4 great empires(Germany, Russia, Ottoman Empire and Austro-Hungary).




Outbreak

On June 28th, 1914, in the capital of Bosnia, Sarajevo, Archduke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the Austrian throne, was assassinated by a member(Gavrilo Princip) of a Serbian terrorist group(Black Hand). This was the spark that would engulf Europe with war for 4 years. Germany had given Austria a blank cheque, which would give their empire unconditional support for whatever action they take to punish Serbia. The Russians had an alliance with Serbia, and France had an alliance with Russia. Italy however, had a treaty with Austria to defend them, not to support them. Austria had given Serbia an outrageous demand, as a way to wage war. During the summer of 1914, Austria would declare war on Serbia, and Germany would declare war on Russia and France.




Outcome

The war caused millions of deaths and caused destructions that still exist today. Many places in Europe are still littered with unexploded ordnance, and shells from artillery. The treaty of Versailles demanded territory, war reparations, and colonies from the Germans, and this treaty would later be the cause of another World War.
