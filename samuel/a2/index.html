Steak and Mash

Instructions

Introduction

This is a recipe for steak and mash. This is my favourite meal because I feel like it is very simple to make and yet it has so much flavour. Steak is way easier than anyone would ever think it is. I would eat it everyday if I could. It is rich and delicious. On top of that, the texture is so tender. This recipe doesn't include greens, but, I would recommend making a salad or cooking some asparagus as a side. This recipe will feed 2, perfect for a date dinner at home. I would recommend having a nice glass of red wine alongside the meal. This recipe uses the reverse-sear method. I hope you enjoy.

 

Ingredients

 You will need:

  • 1 steak of your choice. I recommend ribeye. (Note that different steaks have varying cooking times.)
  • 2-4 gold Yukon potatoes
  • At least 3 tbs butter
  • 2 sprigs of rosemary
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • At least 100ml of milk
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • (Optional)Whatever steak seasoning you want

 

Instructions

Steak:

  1. Preheat oven to 275°F
  2. Season your steak with salt, pepper, and whatever else you want. (Just don't over or under-season.)
  3. Add oil to your steak to make the seasoning stick.
  4. Once the oven is preheated, add the steak into the oven on a heat-safe rack. (Make sure the rack is on a tray to catch juices.)
  5. Cook steak for 30-45 minutes or until it reaches ~120°F internally (Having a meat thermometer is HIGHLY recommended for this step. Make sure to check every 10 minutes)
  6. Once the steak is finished, extract it from the oven and heat a pan on the highest heat you can. (Cast iron is best)
  7. Add oil to the pan and, once it's smoking, add the steak
  8. Cook on each side for 30s - 1 minute or until you get a beautiful golden-brown crust on it (Browning the sides is optional)
  9. Once both sides are browned, turn off the heat and add rosemary, garlic, and 1tbs of butter to the pan.
  10. Baste by tilting the pan and the steak to one side, take a spoon and scoop up a bit of the melted butter and throw it on the steak
  11. Rest on a wooden cutting board for at least 5 minutes
  12. Warm a plate by taking a damp towel, and wrapping a plate with it, microwave it for 30 seconds
  13. Cut steak into thin strips and plate accordingly to the amount of people

Mash:

  1. While steak is cooking, boil 3½ cups of water (Varies depending on the amount of potato you use)
  2. Peel and cut potatoes into 1-inch cubes (They don't have to be cubic just make sure that the sizes are even)
  3. Once water is boiling, season with salt and add potatoes; boil for 12 minutes or until fork tender
  4. Once potatoes are done, drain water and add 2tbs of butter and 100ml of milk, mash potatoes
  5. Add milk and butter until your desired texture and flavour, salt to taste, keep it in the pot on warm until plating stage
  6. Optionally, you can put the potatoes through a ricer and then a fine sieve which will give you the smoothest potatoes ever
