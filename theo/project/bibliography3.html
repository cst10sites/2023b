Frank Lloyd Wright


Frank Lloyd Wright. born in 1867 in Wisconsin as Frank Lincon Wright, to his mother Anna Lloyd Jones, and father William C. Wright. They grew up relatively impoverished which made it a tense situation for a young Frank.


In the 1880s, Wright went to the University of Wisconsin to study engineering, which has relatively close ties with architecture. After dropping out without receiving a degree, Frank went on to work at an architectural firm as a draftsman, helping out wherever he could.


Moving from Wisconsin, Wright went on an expedition to Chicago in search of employment. It was there that Wright found a job as an architectural designer despite little to no previous education.


Despite having a stable job, due to numerous factors including debt, Wright was still relatively poor, so he began to take commissions on the side. This meant he could freely design homes and show more of his architectural style.


At the turn of the century, Wright was beginning to gain popularity and began taking on bigger work. While working in Japan in the late 1910s, Wright designed the Imperial Hotel in Tokyo.


Throughout the first half of the 20th century, Frank Lloyd Wright designed some of his most famous buildings including those that are showcased on this website. And it seems that the later Wright got into his life, the more the public seemed to admire his work.


Wright died on April 9th, 1959, at the age of 91. At the time of his death, Wright designed approximately 400 buildings, around 300 of which still stand today.
